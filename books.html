Book Reviews

Disclaimer:
The rating and comments given below are just my opinions formed while reading these books.
However i acknowledge that writing a book is a very difficult job and i do not have the knowledge, patience, discipline or perservence required to write a book.
So please use your own judgements.

Bootstrapping Microservices with Docker, Kubernetes, and Terraform
A project-based guide

Ashley Davis
March 9, 2021
440 pages

Rating:

Comments:
Very good book that explains these difficult topics in practical terms by means of a scenario. Such books, which combine theory with practical example, are best means of learning.

Kubernetes and Docker - An Enterprise Guide
Effectively containerize applications, integrate enterprise systems, and scale applications in your enterprise

Scott Surovich, Marc Boorshtein
November 6, 2020
526 pages

Rating:

Review comments:
This book is Not for Kubernetes beginner. It is not an easy book to read.
But if your basics of kubernetes are clear and you have been using it in your enterprise, this book gives you a lot of learning and insights from enterprise usage standpoint.

Learn Helm
Improve productivity, reduce complexity, and speed up cloud-native adoption with Helm for Kubernetes

Andrew Block, Austin Dewey
June 10, 2020
344 pages

Rating:

Review comments:
Helm is an integral part of Kubernetes deployments and this is a good book to learn it.
